1HandIndia Seller Guide Registration and approval
Step 1

Register and get approved

Every seller is reviewed before selling starts. This protects customers, improves trust, and keeps product, service, and payout information clean from the beginning.

Registration steps

  1. Open seller registration. Visit /seller/register and sign in with your mobile or email account.
  2. Choose your selling type. Select retail seller, service provider, wholesale supplier, or the option approved for your business.
  3. Add business details. Enter store name, owner name, phone, email, GST or PAN details, business address, and service areas.
  4. Upload documents. Add clear photos or PDFs for identity, business, address, bank, and category certificates where needed.
  5. Wait for admin review. You may see a pending approval page until the admin approves, rejects, or requests correction.
  6. Complete your profile. After approval, open seller center and finish store profile, payout details, products, services, and policies.

Keep these ready

  • Owner or authorized person ID proof.
  • PAN card or business PAN.
  • GST certificate if registered.
  • Business address proof.
  • Bank account proof or cancelled cheque.
  • Store logo, banner, and business description.
  • Product photos or service sample photos.
  • Category license such as FSSAI, repair certificate, trade license, or other required proof.
Approval tip: Use the same business name, phone number, address, and bank holder name across documents where possible. Mismatched details can delay approval.

What approval means

Profile approved Your seller account can access operational seller tools.
Listing review continues Products and services may still need separate admin approval before going public.
Finance starts safely Payout details and settlement records stay visible in seller finance pages.

1HandIndia Seller Guide Operations and money flow
Step 3

Sell, fulfil, and get paid

Retail products, services, and B2B supply have different workflows, but each one follows the same trust pattern: clear listing, correct status updates, verified payment, and visible settlement.

Retail product orders

  1. Create product with images, category, price, stock, and variants.
  2. Admin approves product for storefront visibility.
  3. Customer adds product to cart and places order.
  4. Seller accepts, processes, packs, dispatches, or updates delivery status.
  5. Paid and delivered seller split becomes eligible for settlement.

Service bookings

  1. Create service with price, visit mode, cancellation rules, and service area.
  2. Admin approves service listing for customers.
  3. Customer books service or asks for quote.
  4. Provider accepts, schedules, quotes, starts work, and submits proof.
  5. Completed and verified service becomes eligible for settlement.

B2B enquiries and orders

  1. Business buyer submits product, quantity, location, and requirement details.
  2. Seller reviews enquiry and sends quotation or response.
  3. Buyer confirms the quotation when ready.
  4. Admin verifies, approves, or finalises the B2B workflow.
  5. Seller tracks B2B order, payment, and fulfilment status.

Payments and payouts

  1. Customer payment can use enabled methods such as Razorpay, COD, bank transfer, or manual verification.
  2. Commission, platform deductions, coupon deductions, taxes, and adjustments are recorded transparently.
  3. Seller sees wallet, payout records, ledger entries, and statements.
  4. Eligible sellers can request manual payout where the platform enables it.
  5. Finance or admin approves, rejects, or marks payout as paid with reference details.

1HandIndia Seller Guide Checklist and support
Ready List

First-week seller checklist

Use this checklist after approval so your storefront looks professional and your operations stay clean.

Before going live

  • Store profile is complete with logo, banner, description, and contact details.
  • Business address and service area are correct.
  • Bank or UPI payout details are updated privately.
  • Retail products have clear photos, correct price, stock, MRP, SKU, and variant data.
  • Service listings explain included work, excluded work, price, visit mode, and cancellation policy.
  • Documents and certificates are clear and readable.
  • Phone and email are active for admin or customer communication.

After orders start

  • Check seller dashboard every day.
  • Respond quickly to orders, bookings, B2B enquiries, and admin requests.
  • Update status only when the real work has happened.
  • Keep product stock accurate to avoid cancellations.
  • Use safe packaging for retail orders.
  • Submit service completion proof when required.
  • Review finance wallet, payouts, statements, deductions, and settlement status weekly.

Need help?

Use the website support/contact flow or contact the 1HandIndia operations team. Keep your seller name, registered phone, and order or enquiry number ready.
